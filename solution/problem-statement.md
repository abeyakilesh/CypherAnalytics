# Problem Statement

## The Challenge: Financial Privacy in Real-Time Monitoring

### Background
Financial institutions process billions of transactions daily. Detecting fraud, money laundering, and anomalous behavior in real time is critical for regulatory compliance and customer protection. However, the very systems designed to protect customers face a paradox: **analyzing transactions for fraud requires accessing sensitive financial data, which itself creates privacy risks**.

### The Core Problem
Modern transaction monitoring systems typically face these challenges:

1. **Data Exposure Risk**: Analysts and compliance officers viewing dashboards can see raw account numbers, full names, and detailed transaction histories — creating insider threat vulnerabilities

2. **Regulatory Pressure**: Regulations like GDPR, PCI-DSS, and financial privacy laws require that personally identifiable information (PII) is protected even within monitoring systems

3. **Operational Conflict**: Security teams need enough detail to investigate suspicious transactions, but compliance teams need to minimize data exposure — these goals often conflict

4. **Audit Trail Requirements**: Organizations must demonstrate that sensitive data is protected at every stage of processing, not just at the database level

### Real-World Impact
- **Data breaches at financial institutions** cost an average of $5.9M per incident (IBM 2024)
- **67% of financial data breaches** involve internal access to monitoring systems
- **Regulatory fines** for privacy violations in financial services exceeded $4.7B globally in 2024

### What's Needed
A monitoring platform that can:
- ✅ Detect fraud and anomalies in real time
- ✅ Never expose raw sensitive data in the user interface
- ✅ Encrypt sensitive data at rest
- ✅ Provide role-based access with graduated data visibility
- ✅ Explain how and why each field is protected
- ✅ Maintain a quantifiable privacy protection score

This platform addresses exactly this need — providing **full analytical capability with zero PII exposure** in the monitoring dashboard.
